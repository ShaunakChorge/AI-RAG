## Sample Questions for Testing

### From discharge_instructions.txt
Q: What is the weight limit for lifting after I am discharged?
Expected: You should not lift anything heavier than 10 pounds for the first two weeks.

Q: How soon do I need to schedule a follow-up appointment after discharge?
Expected: You must schedule and attend a follow-up appointment within 7 days of your discharge date.

### From appointment_policy.txt
Q: How early do I need to arrive before my scheduled appointment time?
Expected: You are required to arrive at least 15 minutes prior to your scheduled appointment.

Q: What happens if I miss my appointment without cancelling in advance?
Expected: If you miss an appointment without 24-hour notice, it is considered a "no-show". Three no-shows in a 12-month period may lead to suspension of scheduling privileges.

### From insurance_faq.txt
Q: What should I do if I find an error on my medical bill?
Expected: You must initiate a billing dispute within 30 days by calling Patient Financial Services or submitting a written explanation via the patient portal.

Q: Who is eligible for the financial assistance program?
Expected: Uninsured or underinsured patients may be eligible based on their total household income and family size relative to the Federal Poverty Guidelines.

### From hipaa_guidelines.txt
Q: How long are adult medical records kept by the hospital?
Expected: Adult medical records are securely retained for a minimum of seven years after the last patient encounter.

Q: How soon must patients be notified if there is a data breach?
Expected: Patients must be notified without unreasonable delay, and no later than 60 days following the discovery of the breach.

### From medication_refill_policy.txt
Q: Can I use the online portal to refill a prescription for an opioid painkiller?
Expected: No, controlled substances like opioid pain relievers cannot be refilled via the online portal.

Q: At what point can I request a refill for my medication?
Expected: You cannot request a refill until you have used at least 75% of your previously prescribed supply based on the dosing schedule.

### From telehealth_guidelines.txt
Q: Can I get an initial prescription for a controlled substance during a telehealth visit?
Expected: No, federal guidelines heavily restrict providers from initiating new prescriptions for Schedule II controlled substances without a prior in-person medical evaluation.

Q: Am I allowed to have my telehealth visit while sitting in a coffee shop?
Expected: No, conducting visits in public locations is strictly prohibited to protect your privacy and will result in immediate termination of the appointment.
