Insurance Eligibility and Billing Frequently Asked Questions

This comprehensive guide is designed to help patients navigate our insurance policies, understand complex billing procedures, and clarify financial responsibilities associated with medical care received at our facility.

Accepted Insurance Plans
Our hospital network is committed to providing accessible care by partnering with a wide variety of insurance providers. We currently accept major commercial insurance plans, including Blue Cross Blue Shield, Aetna, Cigna, and UnitedHealthcare. Additionally, we are fully enrolled participating providers for government-sponsored programs, including both standard Medicare and state Medicaid plans. However, provider networks change frequently, so patients are strongly encouraged to verify their specific in-network status by contacting their insurance carrier directly prior to receiving services.

Pre-Authorization Requirements
Many insurance companies require a formal pre-authorization (also known as prior authorization) for non-emergency services, advanced imaging studies such as MRIs and CT scans, elective surgeries, and specialized medical equipment. It is the patient's responsibility to understand their plan's requirements. Our billing department will submit the clinical documentation to your insurer to request pre-authorization; however, if a service is rendered without required pre-authorization, the insurance company may deny the claim, leaving the patient fully responsible for the total cost of the procedure.

Understanding Out-of-Pocket Costs, Copays, and Deductibles
Your financial responsibility is determined by your insurance policy structure. A "copay" is a fixed, predetermined amount that you must pay at the time of service, typically for clinic visits or prescription medications. A "deductible" is the total dollar amount you must pay out-of-pocket for covered health care services before your insurance plan begins to pay. Once your deductible is met, you may still be responsible for "coinsurance," which is a percentage of the total remaining costs. We require all expected copays to be collected at the registration desk before your appointment begins.

What to Do if Insurance is Denied
If your insurance company issues a denial for a submitted claim, our billing department will typically receive an Explanation of Benefits (EOB) outlining the reason. Common reasons for denial include lack of medical necessity, missing pre-authorization, or out-of-network provider usage. If a denial occurs, you have the right to file an appeal directly with your insurance carrier. Our clinical staff can assist by providing supplemental medical records or a letter of medical necessity to support your appeal, but the patient must initiate the formal appeals process.

Billing Dispute Process
If you believe there is an error on your medical bill, such as a duplicate charge or a charge for a service you did not receive, you must initiate the billing dispute process within 30 days of receiving the statement. To formally dispute a charge, please contact our Patient Financial Services department by phone or submit a detailed written explanation via the secure online patient portal. Your account will be placed on an administrative hold, temporarily suspending late fees and collection actions, while our billing specialists investigate the discrepancy. You will receive a written resolution within 45 business days.

Financial Assistance Program
We believe that no patient should be denied medically necessary care due to an inability to pay. Therefore, we offer a comprehensive Financial Assistance Program for uninsured and underinsured individuals. Eligibility is determined based on total household income and family size, relative to the current Federal Poverty Guidelines. Patients may qualify for significantly discounted care or complete debt forgiveness. To apply, patients must submit a completed Financial Assistance Application along with required supporting documentation, such as recent tax returns and pay stubs.
