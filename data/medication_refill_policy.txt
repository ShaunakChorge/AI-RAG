Medication Refill Procedures and Clinical Policies

Ensuring that you have continuous access to your prescribed medications is a critical component of your ongoing healthcare. This document details our standardized clinic procedures regarding medication refill requests to guarantee safety, compliance, and efficiency.

How to Request a Medication Refill
Patients are provided with three primary avenues to request medication refills. The most efficient method is to utilize the secure online patient portal, where you can select the specific medication from your active list and route the request directly to your provider's clinical team. Secondly, you may request a refill by calling our automated prescription refill phone line, available 24 hours a day. Finally, refills may be requested and authorized during a scheduled telehealth consultation with your primary care provider. Please note that we strongly discourage patients from relying on their retail pharmacy to fax refill requests to our clinic, as this often leads to administrative delays.

Information to Have Ready
When submitting a refill request via the automated phone line, you must have specific information readily available to ensure accurate processing. Please be prepared to provide your full legal name, date of birth, your pharmacy’s specific phone number, the exact name of the medication, the current prescribed dosage (e.g., 20 milligrams), and the frequency with which you take the medication. Incomplete information will halt the refill process until the missing details can be verified.

Processing Time
Routine medication refill requests require a standard 72-hour processing time. This 72-hour window allows our clinical staff to thoroughly review your medical chart, verify that you are up-to-date on required laboratory tests, and ensure there are no newly documented drug interactions. We strongly advise patients to initiate their refill requests at least one full week before their current medication supply is entirely exhausted to prevent any dangerous interruptions in therapy.

Early Refill Policy
To prevent medication stockpiling and potential misuse, our clinic enforces a strict early refill policy. Refill requests will not be authorized if less than 75% of the previously prescribed supply has been utilized, based on the prescribed dosing schedule. For example, a 30-day supply cannot be refilled until at least day 23 of the cycle. Exceptions for travel or lost medications require direct verbal approval from the prescribing physician.

Controlled Substance Restrictions
Medications classified as controlled substances by the Drug Enforcement Administration (DEA), such as opioid pain relievers, stimulants for ADHD, and certain anti-anxiety medications, are subject to stringent federal regulations. Controlled substances absolutely cannot be refilled via the online patient portal or the automated phone line. Patients requiring continued treatment with controlled substances must schedule an in-person appointment or an approved telehealth visit for clinical evaluation prior to a new prescription being issued.

Mail-Order Pharmacy Option
For patients taking maintenance medications for chronic conditions such as hypertension or hyperlipidemia, we highly recommend utilizing our affiliated mail-order pharmacy option. This service provides a convenient 90-day supply of medications delivered securely to your residence, often at a reduced out-of-pocket cost compared to standard 30-day retail fills. Please contact our pharmacy liaison to transition your eligible prescriptions to the mail-order service.

Emergency Refill Process
In the event that you completely run out of a vital, non-controlled maintenance medication over a weekend or major holiday, you may utilize the emergency refill process. You can visit your regular retail pharmacy and request an emergency three-day supply. Pharmacists possess the legal discretion to dispense a small emergency supply of life-sustaining medications to bridge the gap until our clinic reopens on the next business day.
