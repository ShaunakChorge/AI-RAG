Hospital Appointment Scheduling Policy

This document outlines the official policies and procedures for scheduling, modifying, and attending medical appointments at our healthcare facility. These guidelines are designed to ensure that all patients receive timely, efficient, and equitable access to necessary medical care. Please review these policies carefully to familiarize yourself with our operational standards.

How to Book an Appointment
Patients may book an appointment through two primary methods. First, appointments can be scheduled online via the secure patient portal, which is accessible 24 hours a day, 7 days a week. The portal allows you to view available time slots and select the most convenient option. Second, appointments may be scheduled by calling the central scheduling office during regular business hours, Monday through Friday, from 8:00 AM to 5:00 PM. Please have your patient ID number and current insurance information ready when calling.

Types of Appointments
When scheduling, please indicate the specific type of appointment required. "New Patient" appointments are required for individuals who have not received care within our network during the past three years. "Follow-up" appointments are designated for existing patients returning to monitor an ongoing condition or review test results. "Urgent Care" appointments are available on a same-day or next-day basis for acute, non-life-threatening medical issues that require prompt attention but do not warrant a visit to the Emergency Department.

Department-Specific Booking Guidelines
Certain specialized departments have unique booking requirements. For Cardiology appointments, patients must obtain a referral from their primary care physician prior to scheduling, and all previous echocardiogram results must be forwarded to our office. Orthopedics appointments require recent X-rays or MRI scans (taken within the last six months) to be uploaded to the patient portal at least two days before the visit. Neurology consultations mandate that patients complete a comprehensive neurological symptom questionnaire, which will be emailed upon booking. General Medicine appointments do not require prior referrals, and patients may self-schedule via the portal at their convenience.

Arriving Early Requirement and What to Bring
All patients are strictly required to arrive at the clinic registration desk at least 15 minutes prior to their scheduled appointment time. This buffer allows sufficient time to complete necessary administrative paperwork, update medical history, and verify insurance coverage. When arriving for your appointment, you must bring a valid, unexpired government-issued photo identification card, your current physical or digital health insurance card, a complete list of all current medications including dosages, and any relevant external medical records or test results.

Cancellation Policy
We require a minimum of 24-hour advance notice for all appointment cancellations or rescheduling requests. This policy allows us to offer the vacated time slot to another patient who may be waiting for medical care. You may cancel your appointment through the secure online patient portal or by calling the central scheduling line. If you leave a voicemail outside of regular business hours, the timestamp of the message will serve as the official notification time.

No-Show Policy
A "no-show" occurs when a patient misses a scheduled appointment without providing the required 24-hour cancellation notice. Our facility enforces a strict no-show policy to maximize access to care for all patients. If a patient incurs three no-shows within a rolling twelve-month period, they may be subject to a review by the clinic administration, which could result in a temporary suspension of scheduling privileges or discharge from the practice. Extenuating circumstances, such as medical emergencies or severe weather events, will be considered on a case-by-case basis.
